# Chinese Astrology: BaZi (Four Pillars of Destiny)

## What BaZi Is

BaZi (八字, "Eight Characters") is the Chinese metaphysical system based on the exact date and time of birth, expressed as four pillars — Year, Month, Day, Hour — each containing a Heavenly Stem (天干) and Earthly Branch (地支). These eight characters describe your elemental constitution, personality, life patterns, and luck cycles.

BaZi is NOT the same as Chinese zodiac (12 animal signs). The zodiac is just the Year Branch — one-eighth of your chart.

## Reading a BaZi Chart

### Step 1: Identify the Day Master
The **Day Stem** = the Day Master = YOU. Everything else in the chart is read in relation to the Day Master.

### Step 2: Assess Day Master Strength
- Is the Day Master supported by the same element in other pillars?
- Is it in season? (Wood is strong in Spring months, weak in Autumn)
- Is it in its own branch? (甲 in 寅 branch = very strong)

**Strong Day Master**: needs output, wealth, or officer stars to balance
**Weak Day Master**: needs resource or companion stars to support

### Step 3: Identify the 10 Gods (十神)
Each element in the chart has a relationship to the Day Master expressed as one of 10 gods:

## Luck Pillars (大運)

BaZi luck pillars run in 10-year cycles, beginning from a starting age calculated from the birth date.

**How to find them:** Use a BaZi calculator — it will show pillars from birth age onward.

Each pillar introduces two new characters (stem + branch) that interact with the natal chart for 10 years. These are the most important timing tool in BaZi.

**Key rule:** The luck pillar doesn't "override" the natal chart — it activates or suppresses elements within it. An unfavorable luck pillar won't destroy a strong chart; a favorable one won't rescue a fundamentally weak one.

## BaZi vs Vedic: Why They Seem to Conflict

**Example:** A person has Dog year in BaZi (suggesting Earth excess, potential health issues related to digestion/earth organs). But their Vedic chart shows strong Jupiter indicating robust health.

**Resolution:** These are not contradictory. BaZi Earth excess describes a **constitutional tendency** toward digestive issues — a vulnerability in the body system. Vedic Jupiter describes the **protective grace** operating in this lifetime. The person may have a digestive system that needs more attention than average (BaZi) but also has strong recovery capacity and will generally be protected from severe illness (Jupiter).

Same person, different layers. Both true simultaneously.
