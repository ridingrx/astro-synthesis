# Vedic / Jyotish Reference

## Key Differences from Western Astrology

1. **Sidereal zodiac** (not tropical): subtract ~23°26' (Lahiri ayanamsa, 1994) from Western positions
2. **Whole-sign houses** (most common in Jyotish): the Ascendant sign = entire 1st house
3. **Focuses on moon sign** as primary identity marker (not Sun sign)
4. **Predictive emphasis**: Dasha systems predict specific life events with remarkable precision
5. **Karma and dharma** are explicit framework — not metaphorical
6. **Remedies (upayas) are integral**, not optional

**Rahu and Ketu** (lunar nodes) are shadow planets — no physical body but enormous karmic weight. Always in opposite signs. Rahu amplifies and obsesses. Ketu releases and spiritualizes.

## Rashis (Signs)

Same 12 signs as Western but sidereal (shifted ~23°). Signs have the same basic meanings but are colored by Jyotish framework — emphasis on dharmic vs adharmic, rajasic/tamasic/sattvic quality.

**Trikona lords** (1st, 5th, 9th house lords): always beneficial, produce good results wherever they sit.
**Kendra lords** (1st, 4th, 7th, 10th house lords): mixed — their nature depends on their natural benefic/malefic status.
**Dusthana lords** (6th, 8th, 12th): challenging house lords — can cause difficulties when placed in kendra/trikona.
**Upachaya houses** (3rd, 6th, 10th, 11th): malefics improve over time in these houses.

## Planetary Dignity (Vedic)

| Planet | Exaltation | Debilitation | Moolatrikona |
|--------|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| Sun | Aries (10°) | Libra (10°) | Leo |
| Moon | Taurus (3°) | Scorpio (3°) | Taurus |
| Mars | Capricorn (28°) | Cancer (28°) | Aries |
| Mercury | Virgo (15°) | Pisces (15°) | Virgo |
| Jupiter | Cancer (5°) | Capricorn (5°) | Sagittarius |
| Venus | Pisces (27°) | Virgo (27°) | Libra |
| Saturn | Libra (20°) | Aries (20°) | Aquarius |

**Neecha bhanga (cancellation of debilitation):** A debilitated planet can be "cancelled" and actually becomes very powerful if:
- The sign lord of the debilitation is in a kendra from lagna or moon
- The exaltation lord of the debilitated planet is in a kendra
This is one of the most important dignity calculations in Jyotish.

**Combustion:** Any planet within 8° of the Sun (varies slightly by planet) is combust — loses significations temporarily. Combust planets are weakened but also spiritualized.

## Vimshottari Dasha System

The most important predictive tool in Jyotish. A 120-year cycle divided by planetary periods:

| Planet | Period |
|--------|--------|
| Ketu | 7 years |
| Venus | 20 years |
| Sun | 6 years |
| Moon | 10 years |
| Mars | 7 years |
| Rahu | 18 years |
| Jupiter | 16 years |
| Saturn | 19 years |
| Mercury | 17 years |

**Starting point:** Based on Moon's nakshatra at birth.

**Antardasha (sub-periods):** Each mahadasha divides into sub-periods of each planet. The mahadasha lord sets the theme; the antardasha lord is the specific flavor.

**How to interpret:**
1. Find current mahadasha (main period) + antardasha (sub-period)
2. Check the mahadasha lord's natal placement, dignity, and house lordship
3. Check what houses the mahadasha lord rules — those life areas will be activated
4. Cross-reference with current transits for timing

**Key ages (approximate for most charts):**
- Saturn mahadasha: often 30s-40s — major karma processing
- Rahu mahadasha: 18 years of ambition, confusion, material obsession, and then insight
- Jupiter mahadasha: expansion, spiritual growth, children, abundance
- Ketu mahadasha: spiritualization, loss of worldly attachments, psychic opening

## Nakshatras (Lunar Mansions)

27 nakshatras divide the zodiac into 13°20' segments. Moon's nakshatra at birth = birth star.

**Why nakshatras matter:**
- More specific than signs — two people with Moon in Scorpio behave very differently if one is Vishakha and the other is Jyeshtha
- Determine dasha starting point
- Each has a deity, symbol, ruling planet, and specific qualities
- Used in muhurta (auspicious timing) and compatibility (kuta matching)

**Key nakshatras for Vish (verify with exact chart):**
With Sun in early Scorpio (sidereal), Sun is likely in **Vishakha** or **Anuradha** nakshatra.
- **Vishakha** (Libra 20° - Scorpio 3°20'): Jupiter-ruled, intense focus on goals, sharp ambition, can be divisive, transformative. Deity: Indra and Agni.
- **Anuradha** (Scorpio 3°20' - 16°40'): Saturn-ruled, deep loyalty, organizational genius, friendships are sacred, struggles with authority. Deity: Mitra.

## Yogas (Special Combinations)

**Rajyogas** (power + success combinations):
- Lord of kendra + lord of trikona conjunct or in mutual aspect = raja yoga
- Multiple raja yogas = significant public achievement

**Dhana Yogas** (wealth):
- 2nd and 11th lord conjunct, mutual aspect, or exchanged
- Jupiter + Venus strong and aspecting 2nd

**Pancha Mahapurusha Yogas:** A planet (Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn) in its own sign or exaltation in a kendra = extraordinary expression of that planet's qualities.

**Kemadruma Yoga:** Moon with no planets in 2nd or 12th from it — emotional isolation, self-reliance by necessity. Can indicate someone who must build their own emotional container.

**Gaja Kesari Yoga:** Jupiter in kendra from Moon — wisdom, recognition, respected figure. Very beneficial.

**Viparita Raja Yoga:** A dusthana lord (6th, 8th, or 12th) placed in another dusthana — paradoxically produces success, often through overcoming crisis.

## Remedies (Upayas) — Overview

> Full remedy details in `references/remedies.md`

**Remedy types in Jyotish:**
1. **Mantra** — specific beej mantras for each graha, recited in multiples of 108
2. **Ratna (gemstone)** — wearing the gemstone of the lagna lord or a benefic planet
3. **Dana (charity)** — donating specific items on the day ruled by the afflicting planet
4. **Upavasa (fasting)** — fasting on the day of the afflicting planet
5. **Havan/Homa** — fire offerings to specific deities or planets
6. **Yantra** — geometric symbols charged for specific planetary energies
7. **Behavioral upayas** — lifestyle changes aligned with the planet's nature
8. **Color therapy** — wearing colors of beneficial planets
9. **Deity worship** — propitiation of the deity associated with the afflicting planet

**Timing for remedies:**
- Start on the day ruled by the relevant planet
- Begin during Shukla paksha (waxing moon) if possible
- Hora (planetary hour) of the relevant planet is ideal
